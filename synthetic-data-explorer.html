Sample Boosting

Lower Risk

Artificially increasing underrepresented parts of a real sample to rebalance quotas or compensate for missing data.

How it works: You collect 500 real responses but only 30 are from rural Gen Z males. A model trained on the real data generates 70 additional synthetic responses for that cell, bringing it to quota.

Validation: ESOMAR LATAM case studies showed average errors of just 1‚Äì3% with this approach. Fairgen guarantees ~3.5x effective sample size increase for segments under 15% of total.

Key insight: This is the safest entry point ‚Äî real data provides the foundation, synthetic data fills gaps.

Fully Synthetic

Higher Risk

Entire datasets built from scratch using models that mimic the real population ‚Äî with little or no human input.

How it works: An LLM is prompted to simulate 1,000 consumers with varied demographics and answer a brand perception survey. No real respondents were involved at any stage.

The danger: Columbia Business School found that only 31% of researchers rated fully synthetic (no real-data training) results as "great." Without real data as an anchor, hallucination risk is extreme.

Key insight: This is where the most dramatic claims and the biggest failures both live.

Click to expand ‚ñº

üîí

Privacy-Preserving

Lower Risk

Transforms real data to protect privacy while maintaining valid statistical properties for analysis.

How it works: A healthcare company has sensitive patient data. A synthetic twin dataset is generated that preserves the correlations and distributions ‚Äî enabling analysis without exposing any individual's identity.

The trade-off: Differential privacy (the gold standard) inevitably reduces data fidelity. Ullman et al. proved no algorithm can simultaneously satisfy differential privacy AND preserve all correlations.

Key insight: The real driver here is regulatory compliance (GDPR, HIPAA), not cost savings.

Digital Twins / Synthetic Personas

Variable Risk

AI-generated respondent profiles capable of interactive engagement ‚Äî virtual consumers you can interview and test concepts against.

How it works: Stanford/Google DeepMind (2024) created AI replicas from 2-hour interviews with 1,052 real participants. These digital twins matched their human counterparts' survey responses with 85% accuracy ‚Äî comparable to humans retesting themselves two weeks later.

The frontier: PersonaPanels creates "Animated Personas" that autonomously browse the internet daily, evolving over time. OpinioAI offers AI-moderated focus groups in hours instead of weeks.

Key insight: The richest and most exciting frontier ‚Äî but accuracy depends entirely on the quality and depth of the underlying real data.

Core Principle: The Fidelity‚ÄìUtility‚ÄìPrivacy Triangle

Every synthetic dataset exists in tension between three dimensions. You cannot simultaneously maximise all three ‚Äî improving one always comes at the cost of another.

üéØ

Fidelity

How faithfully does the synthetic data reproduce the statistical distributions and correlations of real data? Measured via Hellinger Distance, KL Divergence, and pairwise correlation difference.

üõ†

Utility

How useful is the synthetic data for its intended analytical purpose? The standard test: Train on Synthetic, Test on Real (TSTR). Perfect fidelity isn't needed ‚Äî only fit-for-purpose accuracy.

üõ°

Privacy

How well does the data protect individuals in the training set? Differential privacy is the mathematical gold standard, but enforcing it significantly reduces both fidelity and utility.

Where Synthetic Data Breaks ‚Äî Click for Evidence

Critical

Hallucination of Consumer Preferences

Synthetic respondents fabricate brand preferences, purchase behaviour, and consumption patterns that have no basis in reality ‚Äî yet appear statistically plausible.

Documented Evidence

Verasight coffee study: 92% of LLM respondents claimed Dunkin' purchase in the past year ‚Äî the real figure was 47%. Daily coffee drinking was estimated at 91% vs. 56% reality. Peet's regional awareness was overestimated by 25.5 percentage points.

These aren't calibration errors ‚Äî they're wholesale fabrication of consumer behaviour.

Click for evidence ‚ñº

Critical

Bias Amplification

Successive generations of synthetic data amplify model mistakes until minority representations are erased and outdated stereotypes become embedded as "data."

Documented Evidence

FAccT 2024 landmark paper: Chains of generative models "eventually converge to the majority," causing representational disparity between sensitive groups. Minority data is lost first. A 2024 Nature study confirmed models trained on recursively generated data suffer "irreversible defects" ‚Äî a phenomenon called "model collapse" or "Habsburg AI."

Click for evidence ‚ñº

Serious

Systematic Positivity Bias

Synthetic respondents are "people-pleasers" ‚Äî they respond enthusiastically to all concepts, rarely express strong negative opinions, and cluster toward scale midpoints.

Documented Evidence

Emporia Research B2B study: 100% of synthetic respondents chose "Somewhat satisfied" for work-life balance vs. only 45% of real respondents. ACM UMAP 2025: LLM-simulated data "overestimated humans' tendencies to provide positive ratings and exhibited substantially reduced variance." NN/Group: AI respondents have a persistent "people-pleasing" tendency.

Click for evidence ‚ñº

Serious

Inability to Capture Emerging Trends

Synthetic data can only reproduce patterns the model has already learned. It fundamentally cannot surface novel consumer behaviours, unexpected needs, or cultural shifts not yet in training data.

Documented Evidence

B2B International: Accuracy "drops off sharply once we begin to look forward to future behaviour." ZoomRx: Synthetic data "struggles to identify novel patterns not represented in its training data." Conjointly: When asked about a topic outside its training data, an LLM "will produce garbage." At ESRA 2024, predictive results for novel concepts were described as "devastating."

Click for evidence ‚ñº

Caution

False Precision & Overconfidence

Synthetic outputs look statistically robust ‚Äî clean distributions, narrow confidence intervals, consistent patterns ‚Äî but this smoothness masks real-world messiness and creates an illusion of consensus.

Documented Evidence

Bisbee et al. (Cambridge): LLMs produce "distributions that are overly narrow, systematically skewed, or otherwise inconsistent with human survey data." Real humans are messy, contradictory, and unpredictable. When your data is too clean, something is wrong.

Click for evidence ‚ñº

Structural

The Circular Validation Paradox

Every successful validation requires real holdout data ‚Äî which means the cost and time savings are never as dramatic as headlines claim. If you could perfectly predict responses, you wouldn't need synthetic data; if you can't, the data is inherently unreliable.

The Core Tension

ZoomRx articulation: "If an AI system is advanced enough to accurately predict customer responses, why generate synthetic datasets? Conversely, if one must generate synthetic data because the true responses aren't fully known, then the synthetic data is inherently underspecified." This is the fundamental logical problem that no vendor has fully resolved.
